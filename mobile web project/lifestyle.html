Chadwick Boesman

Chadwick Aaron Boseman was born on November 29, 1976 in Anderson, South Carolina, to Carolyn and Leroy Boseman. He aspired to write plays and become an actor and wrote his first play titled Crossroads in his junior year of high school before graduating in 1995. Boseman then entered Howard University in Washington, D.C., earning his Bachelor of Fine Arts degree in directing in 2000.He began his career with guest appearances on television and was a series regular on the short-lived Lincoln Heights (2008â€“2009) and Persons Unknown (2010). Boseman's breakthrough role came as baseball player Jackie Robinson in the biopic 42 (2013). Since then he has continued to portray historical figures such as starring in Get on Up (2014) as singer James Brown, and Marshall (2017) as Supreme Court Justice Thurgood Marshall while also making this character into a historical character Marvel's Black Panther(2018).

Boseman achieved international fame for playing superhero Black Panther in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU) from 2016 to 2019. His Black Panther character played in several Marvel Movies like Capatain America Civil War, Black Panther, Avengers:Infinity War, and Avengers: Endgame. He appeared in four MCU films, including an eponymous 2018 film that earned him an NAACP Image Award for Outstanding Actor in a Motion Picture and a Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Cast in a Motion Picture. Boseman also headlined the film 21 Bridges (2019) and had a supporting role in Da 5 Bloods (2020). His final film, Ma Rainey's Black Bottom, is scheduled to be released posthumously.
