Acting Career

Chadwick Aaron Boseman was born on November 29, 1976 in Anderson, South Carolina. He aspired to write plays, become an actor and wrote his first play in his junior year of high school. Boseman got accepted into Howard University in Washington earned his Bachelor of Fine Arts degree in directing in 2000.

One of Bosemanâ€™s teachers and mentor, actress Phylicia Rashad, was responsible for raising funds to send Boseman and other theater students to attend the Oxford Mid-Summer program at the British American Drama Academy in London. His very first television role was in an episode of NBC's Third Watch in 2003. He then made appearances in Law & Order, CSI:NY, ER and had recurring roles Lincoln Heights.

Chadwick's breakthrough role came as baseball player Jackie Robinson in the biopic 42 released in 2013. Since then he has continued to portray historical figures such as singer James Brown, and as Supreme Court Justice Thurgood Marshall. In 2018 he made this figure into a historical character Marvel's Black Panther(2018). Boseman became the first African American superhero in a major feature film which became the Highest-Grossing Solo Superhero Movie Ever.

Boseman achieved international fame for playing superhero Black Panther in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU) from 2016 to 2019. His Black Panther character played in several Marvel Movies like Captain America Civil War, Black Panther, Avengers:Infinity War, and Avengers: Endgame. He appeared in four MCU films, including an eponymous 2018 film that earned him an NAACP Image Award for Outstanding Actor in a Motion Picture and a Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Cast in a Motion Picture.
